Label theorem

One of the main features of RSM is that anything can be labeled and referenced. For example,

⋮
link tree
〉

Theorem 1. All \(X\) are \(Y\). And now something else. And some other words and math \(\forall X, P(X)\).

And this is the second paragraph, but still in the theorem.

()\[\forall X, P(X),\]

and finally we come to the end.

The first paragraph of the theorem has been labeled and can now be referred to like this. The entire theorem itself can be referred to like this.
